## Overview

Invited lecture at Yokawa Hospital (医療法人社団 敬命会 吉川病院) in Miki, Hyogo, for approximately 60–70 staff members. Systematically covered the full scope of AI adoption for healthcare organizations: from LLM fundamentals and prompt engineering to department-level workflow integration, organizational deployment via Custom GPTs, and information security policy design. Delivered in Japanese.

## Topics

- LLM operating principles and output characteristics (hallucination, context window constraints)
- Prompt engineering: role assignment, output format specification, structured constraints
- Department-level workflow integration: administration (automated minutes formatting, email drafting, internal notice generation), nursing/clinical (simplifying patient IC materials, English literature summarization, conference abstract structuring), education/planning (training material creation, multilingual support)
- Live demo: multi-tone generation of meeting minutes and internal announcements
- Mapping AI application points across the full operational workflow (phased adoption approach)
- Context management and knowledge base construction via Projects
- Quality standardization across the organization through Custom GPTs design and distribution
- Information security: personal/sensitive data handling policies, data control settings, output approval workflows

## Q&A

Also discussed application of other AI tools including CLOVA Note in healthcare settings.
